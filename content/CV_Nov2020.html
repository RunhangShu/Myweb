RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

2019 - Present M.S. thesis project, Drosophila suzukii -microbe interactions (Advisor: Dr. Adam C.N. Wong)

My master research focuses on using insect models and "omics" tools to unravel the effects of gut microbiota on host animals' behaviors and brain functions. For primary lab experiments, I established protocol to generate axenic ("germ-free") D. suzukii, a D. melanogaster relative. I designed and optimized a series of behavioral assays that implemented with camera-tracking system. For NGS data handling, I lead the collaboration between our lab and Neely's lab (University of Sydney) and profiled the single-cell metatranscriptom of fly brains in response to microbiota manipulation. Using Illumina-based 16s rRNA metagenome sequencing, I examined the effects of dietary shift on gut microbial composition of D. suzukii, from where I gained abundant skills of QIIME2 program, downstream statistical analysis and data visualization in R package phyloseq.

2018 - 2019 Undergraduate project (Advisor: Dr. Adam C.N. Wong)

Dissected the chironomid larvae alimentary canal while preserving the internal microbiome for further analysis. Assisting in the dissection of Drosophila brain tissue for single-cell RNA-sequencing.

2016 - 2018 Undergraduate project (Advisor: Dr. Hu Wan)

Examined the sublethal and transgenerational effects of triflumezopyrim on the developmental time, longevity, fecundity and hatchability of brown planthopper. Investigated the effects of elevated-CO2 on the detoxification of brown planthopper, Nilaparvata lugens (Stål).
